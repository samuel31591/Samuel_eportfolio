    Compromising a Medical Mannequin. It has been observed that the pace of technological advancement in the medical industry is faster than the pace of cyber security considerations (Glisson et al., 2015). The critical observation is that a breach of a training tool would result in a clinician receiving incorrect guidance when problem-solving is insightful. A discrepancy between technology and security considerations in this industry can potentially result in a fatality. The studies were conducted on a mannequin model (istan), a wireless patient simulator used at the university's College of Nursing. The device can bleed, secrete bodily fluids, has blood pressure and heart rate, and breathes realistically. The simulator links with iStan software, which controls the mannequin remotely (Still, 2015). By identifying the network security solution and network protocol as the vulnerable components, brute-force attacks against the router PIN, as well as denial-of-service attacks using open-source tools such as Backtrack The tools implemented in this experiment were not sophisticated or difficult to acquire. However, this alone highlights the lack of security currently being implemented in these devices. A brute force attack that targeted the Wi-Fi and a denial-of-service attack rendering the unit inoperable were used to demonstrate the vulnerability of the medical mannequin. A Denial of service (DOS) attack is designed to shut down or make the target unusable. This is accomplished by flooding the target with a large amount of data that the device cannot handle, causing it to become unresponsive. There are several ways to mitigate brute force attacks, such as changing passwords frequently, increasing password length, limiting login attempts, and implementing multifactor authentication. A proactive approach would be setting up alerts for brute force attacks and monitoring and stopping them before they are fully deployed (Varonis, 2021). DOS attacks can be prevented by proactively monitoring a system for any unusual activity by creating a baseline. In addition to this monitoring, an effective method could also be to implement a VPN with MFA required to access the medical device (Phoenixnapp,2018). Over the years, medical devices have gone from being utterly non-connected to being both wirelessly and software controlled. It's worth bearing in mind that there is an international standard to which medical devices must certify; The IEC 62304 Medical device software, which was put in place to prevent cyber-criminal events on medical devices. However, the standard has not been updated to consider and include either network connectivity or cybersecurity (Williams, Woolward, 2015). When medical device manufacturers are required by law to implement strict security measures into the lifecycle of their products, we will likely see the threats above and vulnerabilities decrease.

    Hi Abidemi,

    By disabling WPS, the mannequin can't easily connect to the network, making it more vulnerable. The WiFi Protected Setup (WPS) is a wireless network security standard that makes connections between a router and wireless devices (mannequins) faster and easier. Wireless Protected Setup is a key exchange mechanism rather than a security mechanism. It facilitates connections between devices and access points and is one of the vulnerable points of a network. WPS works with WPA/WPA2/PSK and allows devices to establish connections to WLANs. Utilizing the following methods PIN: Entering a PIN on the device Push-button: A physical or virtual push-button should be pressed on both the access point and the client device during the connection phase. NFC: To use Near Field Communication, the client must bring their device close to the access point. USB drive: Necessary connection information exchange between an access point and a device is done using a USB drive. The short length of WPS PINs and the fact that both PIN parts are validated separately means that an online brute force attack on the PIN can be made within several hours, allowing an attacker to access a WLAN (mannequin device). Wireless LAN clients can authenticate their users and ensure user confidentiality through WPA or WPA2. WPA is vulnerable to the offline dictionary attack since the information required to create and verify a session key is broadcasted (Noureddine Boudriga, 2019, p.270) Using WPA2/enterprise security and mutual client and server authentication provides the most secure and protected wireless network compared to the WPA and WEP.

    Security of Internet of Things (IoT) devices is an ongoing problem. In today's hyper-connected world, security attacks are a reality. Beran points out that a recent Deloitte (2018) report projects that nearly 70% of medical devices will be connected to networks by 2025 (Necat, 2022). Health care products are becoming increasingly connected, and cyber-attacks are becoming more powerful and ingenious, so the threat of cyber-attacks is escalating dramatically. The stakes are high, from revenue losses to product recalls to patient safety and brand damage; or worse, a regulatory body could shut down business operations. In addition, there are the "unknown risks"--cyber vulnerabilities that are ignored due to insufficient forethought or expertise. According to Isioma (Okafor Tolofari, 2022), human factors contribute to cybersecurity breaches. The IoT device like the mannequin can be exploited because of various factors, such as a lack of awareness, negligence, or incorrect access control. Jurbe shed more light on the different regulatory bodies that are poised in ensuring the implementation of security in the production of these medical devices that are connected to the network from the Food and Drug Administration (FDA) provides guidance to help medical devices manufacturers design and maintain products that are cyber secured in the USA, to the computer misuse Act of 1990, which acknowledged that it is an offence to cause a computer to perform any function with the intent to secure unauthorized access to any program or data in England and Wales (Jubet,2022) My initial post described the IEC 62304 regulation on functional safety, which covers software design and maintenance. This regulation describes processes, activities, and tasks to ensure software safety. In medical device software development and maintenance, the software is itself a medical device. There are many security recommendations for IoT devices, including biometrics and two-factor authorization, but blockchain-based IoT security might be the best solution for the largely unregulated IoT market. Blockchain may enhance IoT security, which is most famous for bitcoin and Ethereum. Blockchain technology provides strong protection against data tampering, limiting access to the Internet of Things devices, and removing compromised devices from an IoT network. Regulations related to the use and development of IoT will continue to evolve as they advance. IoT security system on the blockchain is, however, something that seems very promising.

    In this review of the publication of personal information - journalistic exemption, The Data Protection Act of 1988 and 2003 recognises that public interest in free expression and information is a matter of particular concern and allows exemptions for the publication of journalistic, literary, artistic, and academic works where there is a reasonable belief that publication is in the public interest and compliance would be incompatible with the purposes of the work (Glanville, 2018). The data controller must demonstrate that to be exempted from publishing personal data under section 22A of the ACT. Personal data that is processed only for the purposes specified in this subsection is exempt if:

  • (a)the processing is performed to publish any journalistic, literary, or artistic material by a person,

  • (b) considers that the public interest in freedom of expression is of particular importance, and the data controller reasonably believes publication would be in the public interest.

  • (c) based on all the circumstances, the controller believes compliance with that provision is incompatible with the purposes (The National Archives, 2022).

    • This case shows that section 22A of the Acts does not grant absolute immunity that can be routinely invoked to justify publishing unnecessary personal data by publishers and journalists alike. Even though the Commission considered that the Sunday World had a legitimate interest in obtaining and processing information concerning the complainant” prisoner”, it did not feel that identifying them by name or photograph would further enhance or diminish the public interest (Case Studies |Data Protection Commission, n.d.).

    By publishing the complainant's information as research for the article in question, she considered the Sunday World to have contravened Section 2(1)(c). The Sunday World's misuse of the system led to this contravention since excessive processing of the data by publication was not needed to illustrate the point.

As reflected in the essay, man in the middle attack like the Address Resolution

ARP spoofing, also known as ARP poisoning, is a network attack in which a malicious user
sends a
forged ARP (Address Resolution Protocol) message over a local area network. This causes
the
attacker’s Media Access Control (MAC) address to be linked to the IP address of a
legitimate
computer or server on the network (Dacier et al., 2017).
In cybersecurity, there is two mitigation strategy, passive and active mitigation (Daimi
and
Peoples, 2021).
ARP monitoring is a passive mitigation strategy, monitoring or scanning to sniff packets
on
the
Queen’s medical server to detect when ARP is spoofed. When an ARP packet is received,
two
things
must be checked; if they do not match, the server is vulnerable to an ARP spoofing
attack.
We
use Scapy in Python to execute ARP packet detection using MAC addresses and interfaces
to
identify the spoofed ARP packets inside the network. The two things are
Source Media Access Control Address (src MAC) and the sender’s actual Media Access
Control
address.
Scapy is a packet manipulation tool for computer networks. It can forge or decode
packets,
send
them over a network, capture them, match requests, and reply to them. It can also handle
scanning, traceroute, probing, unit testing, attacks, and network discovery.
